Help us train the next generation - free.

SEP is completely free for everyone who attends. That only works because generous people cover what it costs to run it.

Why it matters

SEP is free for every attendee.
That costs something.

We train young people - 16 and up - in practical, income-earning skills across ten tracks: fashion, photography, catering, coding, film-making, and more. No fees. No prerequisites. No catch.

Every seat at SEP is sponsored. Donations fund the materials each participant uses, the equipment we hire for each track, the venue and meals across three full days, and the small grants that help some graduates take their very first step after the event.

What you make possible

Four things your giving
funds directly.

01

Training materials

Every skill track needs real materials - fabrics, camera gear, food ingredients, stationery, tools. Your giving equips participants who couldn't otherwise afford a seat.

02

Venue & logistics

Three days. Thousands of people. The venue, catering, and coordination behind SEP is the biggest cost we carry. Donations keep the doors open and the entry free.

03

Equipment hire

Some tracks need specialist kit - sewing machines, photography lighting, candle-making sets. We hire what each track needs so nobody has to come equipped with anything.

04

Starter support

After the event, some graduates need just a small push to begin - a first batch of fabric, an initial kit, a tool they can't yet afford. Your giving can be the thing that starts them.
